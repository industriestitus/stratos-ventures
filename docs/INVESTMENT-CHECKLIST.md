## Overall Approach

LT GARP 15%+ return over the long term

Buying, holding and maintaining a portfolio of compounding machines with a disciplined and long term approach.

**Compound interest is the eighth wonder of the world never interrupt it unnecessarily.**

I'm waiting for the buying opportunity

4 verticals that I watch Moat, Growth, Valuation, (Un)Certainty

There is not enough time to follow all companies, so I filter them and decide which ones I follow, ideally under 30 

I babysit and monitor my holdings quarterly.

I don't just read, but think about the companies.

I know the risk and control the risk.

I say no to a company as fast as possible.

If I don't want to own a business for 10 years I don't think about owning it for 10 minutes.

Buy good companies, Dont overpay, Do nothing

Small caps perform better

Companies: slow, medium, fast, cyclical, turnaround, asset

Never risk permanent loss!

I'm a business analyst, not a market, macroeconomic, or security analyst
Think forwards and backwards

Invert, always invert

Preparation. Discipline. Patience. Decisiveness

For a quality investor, the window of opportunity arises suddenly and closes quickly. **Be ready and decisive** when it arrives.

**Preparation is everything**. You need to know your companies before an opportunity happens. If not, you will not have the **conviction** to buy when the whole world is selling

I do not sacrifice quality for valuation.

I buy when the company is trading below intrinsic value.

I do not add to companies that are becoming less predictable.

I buy compounding machines.

## Portfolio Rules

I'm selling my losers and adding to my winners.

Does it deserve a place in my portfolio? 

Is it better than my currently owned companies?

I apply smart position sizing, specific buy criteria and specific sell criteria.

## Ideal Company Traits

Monopoly, Wide moat, Organic growth, Strong brand recognition, Easy to understand, Low competition, High pricing power, High demand, Predictable, Low cyclicality, Low capital intensity, Capital light, Cost control, Good management, Reinvestment into the company, Great capital allocation, Returning capital to shareholders, Service based monopoly, Large market share in concentrated industry, High barriers to entry, Long runaway, Large TAM, Scalable, Every revenue dollar increases margin, Operating leverage, Recurring revenue, Strong balance sheet, Minimal financial leverage, Low debt, High profitability, Strong organic growth in revenue fcf and eps, High ROE, High ROIC, High ROCE, High FCF, Low SBC, Growth with operational leverage, Mission critical service, Small cost for the user, Clear organic growth

## What I Avoid

High reinvestment, High R&D, High capex, Risky reinvestment, Undisciplined use of cash flow, Wastefulness, Pet projects, Acquisition based growth, Complex operational risk, Unpredictable cashflows, Intense competition, Dual class share structure, Companies producing commodities, Businesses dependent on commodity prices, Bad industry, High debt, High SBC

## Financial Statement Analysis

### Balance Sheet Thresholds
- Debt/equity < 0.5 (ideal) or < 0.8
- Current ratio > 1.5
- Net debt / FCFF < 5
- Debt/equity < 80%
- Net debt/FCF < 4
- Preferred stock: None
- Retained earnings: growing
- Intangibles < 50% of balance sheet
- Cash > 25% of total debt

### Income Statement Thresholds
- Revenue growth > 5%, ideally > 10%
- Earnings growth > 7%
- EPS growth ideally > 15%
- Gross margin > 40%, ideally > 50%
- Net margin > 10%, ideally > 15%
- Depreciation/gross profit < 10%
- Interest/operating income < 15%
- R&D expense < 30%
- SG&A expense < 30%

### Cash Flow Thresholds
- FCF margin > 15%
- FCF/net income > 80%, ideally > 90%
- Capex/sales < 5%
- Capex/operating cashflow < 15%

### Key Metrics Thresholds
- ROIC > 15%, ideally > 20%
- 5yr P/E < 22.5
- Total LT liabilities / 5yr avg FCF < 5
- EBIT/interest payment > 10

## Buying Criteria

- Intrinsic value determined
- Short thesis written (Why own? When buy? Position size)
- Expected CAGR calculated
- Edge identified
- Position score = CAGR multiplier × Conviction multiplier × Risk multiplier
- 3 strongest bearish arguments documented
- Bear/base/bull cases with probabilities

### Position Scoring Formula
- Expected CAGR: 10-12% → 1x, 12-15% → 1.2x, 15-20% → 1.5x, 20%+ → 2x
- Conviction: Low → 0.5x, Medium → 1x, High → 1.5x, Very High → 2x
- Risk: Very safe → 1.3x, Normal → 1x, Fragile → 0.7x, High risk → 0.5x
- **Position Score = CAGR × Conviction × Risk**
